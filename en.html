Second festival of site-specific art “The Access Point” has been launched on the 15th of June and will run to the 7th of August. The 2016 edition of the festival covers theatre, cinema and public-art.

First festival was held in August-September 2015 and was dedicated to research of St. Petersburg outskirts. Promenade performance in bedroom suburbs, a show in hypermarket, site-based performance at working factory, one-man show in suburbs library – injection of site-specific art to S. Petersburg theatre life was concidered that successful, so it was decided to make Festival annual.

In two years festival became the most representative festival of site-specific art in Russia.

Main aim of the festival is to develop experiment in many areas – audio-visual art, street art, cinema but the central focus is on experimental theatre art. Modern Russian theatre has great potential and its ideas and methodics should be installed into city space: that is the best way of using city reality and at the same time of changing that reality.

This year Festival extends its boundaries and gives two additional programs: cinema and public-art. In the frameworks of cinema program series of open-air show will be presented. Public-art program will present new works of artists from St. Petersburg, Moscow, France and Germany – artists will conquer parks and trade centers, shopping malls and streets of St. Petersburg.

The most intensive program of The Access Point festival – the one dedicated to site-specific theatre - will be opened on 23rd of July and will run to the end of the festival on 7th of August. During two weeks festival will present three own productions as well as seven productions of other theatre companies.

One of the main aims of The Access Point is to give impulse to development of site-specific arts and to explore art potential of St. Petersburg. Because of that The Access Point festival is focused on producing its own productions.

The Access Point 2016 own productions are: Performance in church – “Stalkers” created by Boris Pavlovich and Andrei Mashanov based on the movie of Andrei Tarkovsky; Performance in the shopping mall basement “The Sirens of Titan” directed by Peter Shereshevsky after Kurt Vonnegut; performance on the railway station “Refugee conversations” by Bertold Brecht, directed by Konstantin Uchitel.

St. Petersburg theatres are presenting following productions in the frameworks of the Festival: One-man show in mobile phone shop “Hamlet. Gadget” by Theatre Lusores, Promenade performance “Guide tour 14+” by Etud Theatre, Interactive theatre quest in bread-baking plant “Ligovsky prospect” by Kuryokhin’s Art Center, Social theatre in orthodox church “UnTouchables” created by Golden Mask Laureate Mikhail Patlasov, Performance for teenagers in Communications Museum “Terra One” by Theatre project Vmeste.

In the frameworks of The Access Point off-program will be presented two cross-disciplinary productions: happening “Place-Action” directed by AXE-theatre founder Pavel Semchenko and audio-promenade “Other City” directed by Semen Alexandrovsky.

In 2017 “The Access Point” Festival is planning to break boundaries and extend geography of it’s participants, so we welcome new ideas and collaboration proposals.

We have resources to make our own productions, we could help you to adapt performance in St. Petersburg, we are opened for co-producing. If you have intention to explore colorful and diverse world of Russian modern performing arts contact us immediately.
